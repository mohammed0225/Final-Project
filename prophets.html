Prophets
  • According to Islam, God has sent prophets to humanity to deliver His word at various periods and locations. God has provided wisdom through these selected people since the beginning of time. They were ordinary individuals who taught others about trust in One Almighty God and how to behave in virtue. Certain prophets used Revelation books to reveal Godâ€™s Word.
  • Muslims believe that the prophets provided their people with direction and teaching on worshiping God and living their lives appropriately. Because God is One, His message has remained consistent throughout history. In essence, all prophets preached the Islamic message: Achieve tranquility in your life by submitting to the One Almighty Creator, believing in God, and following His direction.
  • Difference between Prophets and messengers is that: mesessenger is someone who is sent with a new law and scripture. A prophet is sent to follow and verify the law of the messenger before him.
  • Adam is the first Prophet and the first man in Islam
  • Muhammad (PBUH) is the last Prophet and messenger in Islam
  • There are a total of 25 prophets mentioned by name in the Quran
  • Moses (Musa) is the most mentioned prophet (mentioned 143 times)
  • Prophets are chosen by Allah (God) to convey His message to humanity.
  • Prophets are considered the best of human beings and are characterized by righteousness and piety.
  • Islamic tradition recognizes numerous prophets, including Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad, among others.
  • Prophets are role models for Muslims, and their lives serve as examples of how to live a righteous and God-conscious life.
  • Prophets received divine revelations through angels, dreams, or direct communication with Allah.
  • The Quran, the holy book of Islam, mentions the stories and teachings of several prophets, providing guidance and lessons for believers.
  • Each prophet was sent to a specific community or nation, speaking in their own language and addressing their unique circumstances.
  • Prophets faced various challenges, opposition, and hardships during their mission, but they remained steadfast in their faith and devotion to Allah.
  • Islam teaches that prophets were sinless and protected from major sins, but they were not divine or gods to be worshipped.
